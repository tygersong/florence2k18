Crossroads

Explore more in a museum

Crossroads entices museum visitors to interact with more than just a museum's most "famous" offerings. By revealing relationships between the artworks in a museum, Crossroads leads visitors to explore a variety of artworks and their historical connections.

Crossroads is an installation that will connect visitors from famous artworks to other artworks in the museum.

On the floor near a famous artwork, there will be color-coded indicators with phrases such as “Feuded with” or “Born in same town as”. Each color represents a different category. Through lines, these indicators connect with corresponding polygons and colors to bring the visitor to other pieces in the gallery.

Travel down unknown roads in museums

When visitors encounter intersecting paths on their way from famous artworks to other artworks, they are offered the option of taking alternate paths. At these intersections, further connections (such as "In love with" or "Same last name as") expand the opportunities that museum visitors have in exploring the museum's artworks.
